NBA Tanking

The NBA is becoming more known nowadays…mostly for its recent problem with team tanking. The 2025-2026 season has ten teams with below a 40% winrate, the most teams under said win percentage in the 2020s so far. Some teams are just in really bad spots, and can’t really succeed despite putting in winning effort. Other team’s are and have been accused of tanking, with the hopes of gainning better draft picks to help their teams build up faster. While tanking seems to be the hottest topic of the NBA currently, it's been present in the sport for a while.

In the NBA, tanking began to be combated after the result of the 1984 draft. As the draft was coming up, the Houston Rockets intentionally bench their starters with the 1984 draft on the horizon. Houston selected 12-time NBA All-Star Hakeem Olajuwon with the No. 1 overall pick”(Edmonds). The way the draft worked was that the worst team in each conference would flip a coin for the 1st pick, and all the other teams received picks based on the reverse order of their records. This heavily incentivized teams to be the worst possible to have a guaranteed top 2 pick. The Rockets tanking out in the open to abuse this fact was the final straw for the NBA, leading to the creation of the lottery system which eventually got updated to a weighted lottery system. In this system, the first couple of picks would be given to nonplayoff teams. These team would receive a set amount of tickets based on how bad they are, with the worse teams getting more tickets, giving them better odds of getting a higher draft pick.

This current system still incentives tanking, even after updating team pick odds to have the 3 worse teams have the same pick odds in 2019. Although it helps the bad teams rebuild easier while making the picks more random, being a nonplayoff team still gives you a higher draft pick than almost half the other teams. The Oklahoma City Thunder has used tanking in recent years to become a better franchise during the 2021-2022 season. In the final week of the season, the Thunder signed Georgios Kalaitzakis, Melvin Frazier and Zavier Simpson to their roster, all players coming from the league under the NBA: the G league. The organization gave all of them 40 out of the 48 minutes of playtime in a game for the teams final four games. This resulted in the Thunder being ”outscored by 85 points in Kalaitzakis' court time, 92 in Frazier's and 95 in Simpson's. Those final three blowouts kept the Thunder at 24 wins, one behind the Indiana Pacers, clinching the league's fourth-worst record. On lottery night, the Thunder jumped to second and the Pacers dropped to sixth”(Slater). Because of the tanking the Thunder did for their rebuild, they were able to build a title clashign roster by 2025 and actually win a title in the process.

Another example of tanking currently would be a statistic to come out of this season. The nine worst teams in the NBA have all combined for a record of 17 wins to 144 losses when facing, at minimum, play-in teams since the All Star break. Since the All Star Break, each team has played around 15 to 20 games. Displayed within this section is the team with the best record vs the team with the worst.

Even if it’s been a problem for a while, tanking doesn’t seem to be going anytime soon. It’s unfair not only to other teams, but also the fans for having to watch teams underperform on purpose. Tanking can be reduced, but I’m not sure if it will ever be solved.
